## Chapter 33: Health Economics

### Section A: Health Economics Principles

#### Health as an Economic Commodity

**Characteristics of Healthcare:**
- **Indivisibility**: Services cannot be divided
- **Externality**: Benefits extend beyond individual
- **Uncertainty**: Demand unpredictable
- **Information Asymmetry**: Provider-patient knowledge gap
- **Merit Good**: Benefits society beyond individual consumption

#### Demand and Supply in Healthcare

**Healthcare Demand Determinants:**
- **Price**: Healthcare affordability
- **Income**: Purchasing power
- **Insurance Coverage**: Risk pooling
- **Education**: Health awareness
- **Age Structure**: Demographic factors

**Healthcare Supply Factors:**
- **Healthcare Infrastructure**: Facilities and equipment
- **Human Resources**: Doctors, nurses, paramedical staff
- **Technology**: Advanced diagnostic and treatment methods
- **Financing**: Healthcare funding mechanisms
- **Regulation**: Quality and safety standards

**Market Equilibrium Issues:**
- **Information Asymmetry**: Provider knowledge advantage
- **Moral Hazard**: Insurance-induced overuse
- **Adverse Selection**: High-risk individuals seeking insurance
- **Externalities**: Public health benefits

#### Cost-Effectiveness Analysis

**Economic Evaluation Methods:**
1. **Cost-Minimization Analysis**: Compare costs of equally effective alternatives
2. **Cost-Effectiveness Analysis**: Compare costs to health outcomes
3. **Cost-Utility Analysis**: Include quality of life measures
4. **Cost-Benefit Analysis**: Compare costs to monetary benefits

**Key Measures:**
- **Cost-Effectiveness Ratio**: Cost per quality-adjusted life year
- **Incremental Cost-Effectiveness**: Additional cost for additional benefit
- **ICER Threshold**: Willingness to pay for additional health benefit

#### Health Financing Mechanisms

**Financing Methods:**
1. **General Tax Revenue**: Government funding
2. **Social Health Insurance**: Contribution-based systems
3. **Private Health Insurance**: Market-based insurance
4. **Out-of-Pocket Payment**: Direct payment by patients
5. **Community Financing**: Local health insurance schemes

**Financing Principles:**
- **Equity**: Fair distribution of costs
- **Efficiency**: Optimal resource utilization
- **Sustainability**: Long-term financial viability
- **Quality**: Value for money

#### Healthcare Market Failures

**Types of Market Failures:**
1. **Public Goods**: Non-rivalrous and non-excludable services
2. **Externalities**: Spillover benefits or costs
3. **Information Asymmetry**: Imperfect information
4. **Monopoly Power**: Provider concentration
5. **Merit Goods**: Under-provision despite high value

**Government Interventions:**
- **Regulation**: Quality and safety standards
- **Subsidies**: Reduced cost for essential services
- **Public Provision**: Government healthcare facilities
- **Insurance**: Risk pooling mechanisms

### Section C: Health Technology Assessment

#### HTA Methodology

**HTA Definition**: "Systematic evaluation of properties, effects, and/or impacts of health technology"

**HTA Process:**
1. **Scoping**: Define assessment boundaries
2. **Evidence Collection**: Literature and data review
3. **Economic Evaluation**: Cost-effectiveness analysis
4. **Budget Impact Analysis**: Financial implications
5. **Recommendation**: Policy guidance

**HTA Components:**
- **Clinical Effectiveness**: Health outcomes evidence
- **Cost-Effectiveness**: Economic efficiency
- **Safety Profile**: Risk assessment
- **Social Impact**: Equity and accessibility
- **Ethical Considerations**: Value and acceptability

#### Economic Evaluation Methods

**Cost-Effectiveness Analysis (CEA):**
- **Measure**: Cost per unit of health outcome
- **Units**: Life years saved, cases prevented
- **Application**: Comparison of interventions
- **Limitation**: Single outcome measure

**Cost-Utility Analysis (CUA):**
- **Measure**: Quality-Adjusted Life Years (QALYs)
- **Advantage**: Multi-dimensional outcomes
- **Application**: Broad healthcare interventions
- **QALY Calculation**: Length × Quality of life

**Cost-Benefit Analysis (CBA):**
- **Measure**: Monetary costs and benefits
- **Challenge**: Monetary valuation of health
- **Application**: Policy decisions
- **Method**: Willingness to pay analysis

#### Budget Impact Analysis

**Purpose**: "Assess the financial consequences of adopting a new health technology"

**BIA Components:**
- **Population**: Target population identification
- **Utilization**: Expected usage rates
- **Costs**: Direct and indirect costs
- **Time Frame**: Short and long-term projections

**Budget Impact Factors:**
- **Prevalence**: Disease burden in population
- **Treatment Patterns**: Current vs. new technology
- **Market Share**: Adoption rates
- **Cost Structure**: Fixed vs. variable costs

**Policy Applications:**
- **Reimbursement Decisions**: Insurance coverage
- **Formulary Decisions**: Drug listing
- **Procurement Planning**: Bulk purchasing
- **Resource Allocation**: Budget prioritization

#### Priority Setting in Healthcare

**Priority Setting Methods:**
1. **Cost-Effectiveness**: Maximize health outcomes

3. **Severity Assessment**: Disease burden
4. **Innovation Value**: Novel technology benefits

**Priority Setting Principles:**
- **Transparency**: Clear criteria and process
- **Stakeholder Participation**: Inclusive decision-making
- **Evidence-Based**: Scientific evidence integration
- **Accountability**: Responsibility mechanisms
- **Flexibility**: Adaptive to changing needs

**India's Health Technology Priorities:**
- **Essential Medicines**: Cost-effective drug selection

1. **Integration of Preventive and Curative Services**
2. **Three-Tier Healthcare System**: Primary, Secondary, Tertiary

## Case Studies of Successful Health Programs

### Case Study 1: Polio Eradication Program

**Challenge:**
- High polio burden with 500,000 cases annually (1985)
- Virus circulation in multiple states
- Poor sanitation and hygiene conditions

**Strategy Implemented:**
1. **National Polio Surveillance Project**: WHO-NGO partnership
2. **Mass Immunization Campaigns**: NIDs (National Immunization Days)
3. **Micro-planning**: Door-to-door campaigns
4. **Community Mobilization**: ASHA worker involvement
5. **AFP Surveillance**: Acute Flaccid Paralysis monitoring

**Results Achieved:**
- **Last Case**: January 2011
- **WHO Certification**: March 2014
- **Sustained Zero Cases**: 12+ years
- **Global Recognition**: India certified polio-free

**Key Success Factors:**
- **Strong Leadership**: Political commitment
- **International Partnerships**: WHO and UNICEF support
- **Community Participation**: Local volunteer engagement
- **Innovative Strategies**: Oral polio vaccine effectiveness
- **Robust Surveillance**: Real-time monitoring

### Case Study 2: Mission Indradhanush

**Challenge:**
- **Immunization Gaps**: 1.1 million partially vaccinated children
- **Disadvantaged Communities**: Focus on hard-to-reach populations
- **Vaccine Hesitancy**: Community resistance to immunization

**Strategy Implemented:**
1. **Intensive Mission**: 4 rounds of intensified vaccination
2. **Targeted Approach**: Focus on backward districts
3. **Special Day Campaigns**: Pre-planned vaccination dates
4. **Cold Chain Strengthening**: Equipment and logistics
5. **Community Mobilization**: Local leader involvement

**Results Achieved:**
- **Coverage Improvement**: 6.7% increase in full immunization
- **District Coverage**: 528 districts with >90% coverage
- **Beneficiaries**: 2.6 crore children reached
- **Maternal Coverage**: 74 lakh pregnant women vaccinated

**Key Success Factors:**
- **Multi-agency Coordination**: Central and state collaboration
- **Data-driven Planning**: Coverage gap analysis
- **Community Ownership**: Local participation
- **Innovation**: Mobile vaccination teams
- **Monitoring**: Real-time progress tracking

### Case Study 3: National AIDS Control Program

**Challenge:**
- **High HIV Prevalence**: 0.69% in 2000
- **Rapid Epidemic Spread**: High-risk populations affected
- **Stigma and Discrimination**: Social barriers to care

**Strategy Implemented:**
1. **Behavior Change Communication**: Targeted interventions
2. **Safe Blood Supply**: Blood safety measures
3. **Condom Promotion**: Social marketing
4. **Injection Safety**: Needle and syringe programs
5. **Antiretroviral Therapy**: Free treatment program

**Results Achieved:**
- **Prevalence Decline**: 0.69% to 0.21% (2000-2023)
- **New Infections Reduction**: 46% decline since 2010
- **ART Coverage**: 82% of eligible patients
- **Mother-to-Child Transmission**: Reduced to 27%

**Key Success Factors:**
- **Multi-sectoral Approach**: Cross-sector coordination
- **Evidence-based Planning**: Surveillance and monitoring
- **Civil Society Engagement**: NGO partnerships
- **International Support**: Global fund assistance
- **Political Commitment**: Long-term policy support
