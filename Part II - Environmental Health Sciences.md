#### Water Quality Parameters
1. **Physical Parameters**
   - **Turbidity**: Should be <1 NTU

#### Water-Borne Diseases
1. **Bacterial Diseases**
   - **Cholera** (Vibrio cholerae)
     - Incubation: 2-72 hours
     - Symptoms: Watery diarrhea, dehydration
     - Prevention: Safe water, sanitation, vaccination
   
   - **Typhoid Fever** (Salmonella typhi)
     - Incubation: 7-14 days
     - Symptoms: Fever, headache, rose spots
     - Prevention: Water treatment, vaccination

     - Incubation: 1-3 days
     - Symptoms: Bloody diarrhea, abdominal pain
     - Prevention: Hygiene, safe water

   - **Hepatitis A**
     - Incubation: 2-6 weeks
     - Symptoms: Jaundice, fever, fatigue
     - Prevention: Vaccination, safe water

   - **Poliomyelitis**
     - Incubation: 7-14 days
     - Symptoms: Paralysis, fever
     - Prevention: OPV/IPV vaccination

3. **Parasitic Diseases**
   - **Amoebic Dysentery** (Entamoeba histolytica)

     - Chemical: Chlorine, industrial chemicals
   - **Health Significance**: Unacceptable taste may reduce consumption

### ðŸ­ WATER PURIFICATION PROCESSES

#### Conventional Water Treatment Process

**1. Coagulation and Flocculation**
- **Purpose**: Remove suspended particles and colloids
- **Chemicals Used**:
  - Alum (Aluminum sulfate): Most common
  - Ferric chloride: Alternative coagulant
  - Polyaluminum chloride: Advanced coagulant
- **Process**:
  - Rapid mixing: 1-3 minutes
  - Slow mixing: 15-30 minutes
  - Floc formation and settling

**2. Sedimentation**
- **Purpose**: Remove flocculated particles by gravity
- **Types**:
  - Plain sedimentation: For large particles
  - Accelerated sedimentation: With coagulants
- **Design Parameters**:
  - Overflow rate: 1,000-2,000 L/mÂ²/day
  - Retention time: 2-4 hours
  - Settling velocity: 1-3 m/hour

**3. Filtration**
- **Purpose**: Remove remaining suspended particles
- **Types**:
  - **Rapid Sand Filtration**: 
    - Sand depth: 0.6-0.8 m
    - Filtration rate: 3-6 mÂ³/mÂ²/hour
    - Backwashing: Every 24-72 hours
  
  - **Slow Sand Filtration**:
    - Sand depth: 1-1.2 m
    - Biological layer (schmutzdecke)
    - Filtration rate: 0.1-0.3 mÂ³/mÂ²/hour
    - Longer operation (weeks to months)

**4. Disinfection**
- **Purpose**: Kill pathogenic microorganisms
- **Primary Disinfectants**:
  - **Chlorine**: 
    - Dosage: 0.2-0.5 mg/L residual
    - Contact time: 30 minutes
    - CT value: >15 mgÂ·min/L
  
  - **Ozone**:
    - Strong oxidant
    - No residual disinfection

    - Physical disinfection

  - Chloramine formation

**1. Reverse Osmosis (RO)**
- **Principle**: Semi-permeable membrane separation
- **Applications**: Desalination, dissolved solids removal
- **Pressure Required**: 55-70 bar (seawater)

**2. Activated Carbon Filtration**
- **Purpose**: Organic matter, taste, odor removal
- **Types**:
  - Granular activated carbon (GAC)

- **Purpose**: Heavy metal removal, water softening

  - Nitrate removal

  - Chromium removal
- **Resin Types**: Cation, anion exchange

**1. Boiling**
- **Effectiveness**: 100% bacterial and viral inactivation

- **Effectiveness**: Bacteria, viruses, parasites
- **Cost**: Very low
