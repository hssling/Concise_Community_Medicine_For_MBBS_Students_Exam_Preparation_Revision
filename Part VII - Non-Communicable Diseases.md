# Part VII: Non-Communicable Diseases

## Chapter 29: Non-Communicable Diseases - Epidemiology and Control

### Section A: Overview of NCDs

#### Global Burden of NCDs
- **NCDs account for 71% of all deaths globally** (41 million deaths annually)
- **4 major NCDs**: Cardiovascular diseases (17.9M), cancer (9.3M), respiratory diseases (4.1M), diabetes (1.6M)
- **80% of NCD deaths occur in low and middle-income countries**
- **Economic impact**: USD 47 trillion by 2030 if unchecked

#### Classification of NCDs (4x4 Framework)

**WHO 4x4 Framework**:
| 4 Major NCDs | 4 Shared Risk Factors |
|--------------|----------------------|
| Cardiovascular diseases | Tobacco use |
| Cancer | Unhealthy diet |
| Diabetes | Physical inactivity |
| Chronic respiratory diseases | Harmful use of alcohol |

**4 by 4 NCD Framework**:
- **4 Diseases**: CVD, Cancer, Diabetes, CRD
- **4 Risk Factors**: Tobacco, Alcohol, Physical inactivity, Unhealthy diet
- **4 Time frames**: 2025-2030
- **4 Global targets**: 25% reduction in premature mortality, 30% reduction in tobacco use, 10% reduction in physical inactivity, 30% reduction in salt intake

#### Risk Factors (Shared Modifiable Risk Factors)

**Primary Risk Factors**:
1. **Tobacco Use**: 1.1 billion smokers globally, 8 million tobacco-related deaths
2. **Unhealthy Diet**: High salt, sugar, fat intake; low fruit/vegetable consumption
3. **Physical Inactivity**: 60% of adults globally insufficiently active
4. **Harmful Alcohol Use**: 3 million deaths annually

**Intermediate Risk Factors**:
- Raised blood pressure
- Raised blood glucose
- Overweight and obesity
- Dyslipidemia

#### Social Determinants of NCDs

**WHO Commission on Social Determinants**:
1. **Social Gradient**: Higher SES = Lower NCD risk
2. **Social Exclusion**: Marginalized populations affected more
3. **Stress**: Chronic stress increases NCD risk
4. **Living Conditions**: Housing, water, sanitation impact NCDs
5. **Healthcare Access**: Early detection and treatment crucial

**Key Social Determinants in India**:
- **Poverty**: 21.9% below poverty line (2011-12)
- **Education**: 74% literacy rate, gender gap persists
- **Gender**: Women face multiple vulnerabilities
- **Rural-Urban**: Urban areas have higher NCD prevalence
- **Caste**: Dalits have higher NCD mortality rates

#### Epidemiological Transition in India

**Stages of Transition**:
1. **Pre-transition**: High infectious disease burden
2. **Early transition**: Falling infections, rising NCDs
3. **Late transition**: Dominated by NCDs
4. **Post-transition**: Controlled NCDs, aging population

**Current Status in India**:
- **Burden shift**: 62% deaths due to NCDs (2019)
- **Triple burden**: Malnutrition, infections, NCDs
- **Regional variation**: Urban 65%, rural 45% NCD deaths
- **Age transition**: Earlier onset in middle age

**Demographic and Epidemiological Transition**:
- **Population aging**: 10% above 60 years by 2025
- **Urbanization**: 68% urban population by 2030
- **Lifestyle changes**: Westernized diet, sedentary work
- **Environmental changes**: Air pollution, climate change

### Section B: Cardiovascular Diseases

#### Coronary Artery Disease (CAD) Epidemiology

**Global Burden**:
- **#1 cause of death globally**: 17.9 million deaths annually
- **Leading cause of death in India**: 28% of all deaths
- **Projected to increase**: 23 million deaths by 2030

**Indian Statistics**:
- **Prevalence**: 2-8% in urban, 1-4% in rural areas
- **Age-standardized death rate**: 272 per 100,000 population
- **Urban vs Rural**: 3-5 times higher in urban areas
- **Gender**: Male mortality 2-3 times higher than females

**Risk Factors for CAD**:
- **Non-modifiable**: Age, male gender, family history, genetic factors
- **Modifiable**: Smoking, hypertension, diabetes, dyslipidemia, obesity, sedentary lifestyle

#### Stroke and Cerebrovascular Accidents

**Epidemiology**:
- **Global**: 6.2 million deaths annually, 80 million survivors
- **India**: 2nd leading cause of death (1.3 million deaths annually)
- **Prevalence**: 0.5-1% in Indian population
- **Age**: 70% cases above 65 years, but increasing in younger population

**Types of Stroke**:
1. **Ischemic stroke**: 85% of cases
2. **Hemorrhagic stroke**: 15% of cases
3. **TIA**: Transient ischemic attack, warning sign

**Risk Factors**:
- **Non-modifiable**: Age, sex, race, family history
- **Modifiable**: Hypertension (most important), diabetes, smoking, atrial fibrillation, dyslipidemia

**BE-FAST Warning Signs**:
- **B**alance: Sudden loss of balance
- **E**yes: Sudden vision loss
- **F**ace: Facial drooping
- **A**rms: Arm weakness
- **S**peech: Slurred speech
- **T**ime: Time to call emergency services

#### Hypertension (Prevalence, Control, JNC-8 Guidelines)

**Global Burden**:
- **1.28 billion adults** have hypertension (2019)
- **46% adults unaware** of their condition
- **42% diagnosed and treated**
- **21% controlled** to target levels

**Indian Statistics**:
- **Prevalence**: 25-30% in urban, 15-20% in rural areas
- **Total hypertensive patients**: 220-250 million
- **Awareness**: 50-60% of hypertensives aware
- **Treatment**: 40-50% receiving treatment
- **Control**: Only 15-20% achieve target control

**JNC-8 Guidelines (2014)**:

**Treatment Targets**:
- **General population <60 years**: <140/90 mmHg
- **General population ≥60 years**: <150/90 mmHg
- **Diabetes/CKD**: <140/90 mmHg
- **General population ≥60 years with diabetes/CKD**: <140/90 mmHg

**JNC-8 Algorithm**:
1. **Age ≥60 years**: Start treatment at ≥150/90 mmHg, goal <150/90 mmHg
2. **Age <60 years or any age with diabetes/CKD**: Start treatment at ≥140/90 mmHg, goal <140/90 mmHg

**Initial Drug Therapy**:
- **General non-black population**: Thiazide, ACE inhibitor, ARB, or CCB
- **General black population**: Thiazide or CCB
- **Diabetes**: ACE inhibitor or ARB
- **CKD**: ACE inhibitor or ARB

#### Risk Assessment Tools

**Framingham Risk Score**:
- **Purpose**: 10-year cardiovascular risk prediction
- **Variables**: Age, sex, total cholesterol, HDL, smoking, SBP, treatment for hypertension
- **Risk categories**: Low (<10%), intermediate (10-20%), high (>20%)
- **Limitations**: Developed in Caucasian population

**ASCVD Risk Calculator (Pooled Cohort Equations)**:
- **Variables**: Age, race, sex, total cholesterol, HDL, SBP, hypertension treatment, smoking, diabetes
- **Advantage**: Includes wider range of ethnic groups
- **Categories**: Low (<7.5%), intermediate (7.5-20%), high (>20%)

**Indian Risk Assessment**:
- **Interheart study**: 9 modifiable risk factors account for 90% of risk
- **Risk factors**: Smoking, dyslipidemia, hypertension, diabetes, abdominal obesity, stress, diet, physical activity, alcohol

#### Prevention Strategies

**Primordial Prevention**:
- **Target**: Prevent development of risk factors
- **Population approach**: Health promotion, policy changes
- **Examples**: Tobacco control, healthy diet promotion, physical activity promotion

**Primary Prevention**:
- **Target**: Individuals with risk factors but no disease
- **High-risk approach**: Risk assessment and intervention
- **Population approach**: Mass awareness, screening programs

**Secondary Prevention**:
- **Target**: Patients with established disease
- **Objectives**: Prevent progression and complications
- **Components**: Regular follow-up, medication adherence, lifestyle modification

**Tertiary Prevention**:
- **Target**: Patients with complications
- **Objectives**: Prevent disability, improve quality of life
- **Components**: Rehabilitation, palliative care

### Section C: Diabetes Mellitus

#### Type 1 and Type 2 Diabetes

**Type 1 Diabetes**:
- **Cause**: Autoimmune destruction of pancreatic β-cells
- **Age of onset**: Usually in children/young adults
- **Prevalence**: 5-10% of all diabetes cases
- **Insulin therapy**: Required for survival

**Type 2 Diabetes**:
- **Cause**: Insulin resistance and β-cell dysfunction
- **Age of onset**: Usually >40 years, but increasingly in younger
- **Prevalence**: 90-95% of all diabetes cases
- **Risk factors**: Obesity, family history, sedentary lifestyle, ethnicity

#### Prevalence and Trends in India

**Current Prevalence**:
- **National Urban Diabetes Study (2000)**: 12.1% in urban areas
- **Rural surveys**: 2-4% prevalence
- **Diabetes belt**: Urban areas of Punjab, Tamil Nadu, Kerala, Goa
- **Total cases**: 77 million (2019), projected 134 million by 2045

**Trends**:
- **Increasing prevalence**: From 7.1% (2009) to 10.4% (2019)
- **Age shift**: Earlier onset, affecting productive age groups
- **Urban-rural gap**: Narrowing due to urbanization
- **Geographic variation**: Southern states have higher prevalence

**Risk Factors in Indian Population**:
- **Genetic**: Higher susceptibility to insulin resistance
- **Anthropometric**: Higher central obesity, lower BMI
- **Lifestyle**: Rapid transition to sedentary lifestyle
- **Diet**: High carbohydrate intake, low fiber

#### Complications

**Microvascular Complications**:
1. **Diabetic Retinopathy**:
   - **Prevalence**: 18-22% in diabetics
   - **Types**: NPDR, PDR, DME
   - **Screening**: Annual retinal examination
   - **Prevention**: Glycemic control, BP control

2. **Diabetic Nephropathy**:
   - **Progression**: Normo → micro → macroalbuminuria
   - **Prevalence**: 20-30% of type 1, 10-20% of type 2
   - **Prevention**: ACE inhibitors, glycemic control

3. **Diabetic Neuropathy**:
   - **Types**: Sensory, motor, autonomic
   - **Prevalence**: 30-50% of diabetics
   - **Screening**: Annual foot examination

**Macrovascular Complications**:
1. **Coronary Artery Disease**: 2-4 times higher risk
2. **Stroke**: 2-3 times higher risk
3. **Peripheral Artery Disease**: Increased risk of amputation

#### Screening and Diagnosis

**ADA Criteria (2024)**:

**Diagnostic Tests**:
1. **HbA1c**: ≥6.5% (48 mmol/mol)
2. **Fasting plasma glucose**: ≥126 mg/dL (7.0 mmol/L)
3. **2-hour plasma glucose**: ≥200 mg/dL (11.1 mmol/L) during OGTT
4. **Random plasma glucose**: ≥200 mg/dL (11.1 mmol/L) with symptoms

**WHO Criteria**:
- Similar to ADA but emphasizes community screening
- Additional focus on gestational diabetes screening

**Screening Recommendations**:
- **Universal screening**: All adults ≥35 years
- **High-risk individuals**: Earlier and more frequent screening
- **Frequency**: Every 3 years if normal, annually if high-risk

**Risk Assessment Tools**:
- **Indian Diabetes Risk Score (IDRS)**: 
  - Parameters: Age, abdominal obesity, physical activity, family history
  - Cut-off: >60 = high risk, 30-60 = moderate risk, <30 = low risk

#### Management and Prevention

**Diabetes Management Targets**:
- **HbA1c**: <7% (individualized)
- **Blood pressure**: <140/90 mmHg (some guidelines <130/80 mmHg)
- **LDL cholesterol**: <100 mg/dL (high risk <70 mg/dL)
- **BMI**: <23 kg/m² for Asians

**Prevention Strategies**:
1. **Primary Prevention**:
   - Lifestyle modification: Diet and exercise
   - Metformin in pre-diabetes
   - Population-based interventions

2. **Secondary Prevention**:
   - Early detection and treatment
   - Complication screening
   - Risk factor modification

3. **Tertiary Prevention**:
   - Prevent progression of complications
   - Rehabilitation services
   - Quality of life improvement

### Section D: Cancer

#### Cancer Epidemiology in India

**Global Burden**:
- **19.3 million new cases** annually (2020)
- **10 million deaths** annually
- **Most common**: Lung, breast, colorectal, prostate, stomach

**Indian Statistics**:
- **New cases**: 1.4 million annually (2020)
- **Deaths**: 0.85 million annually
- **Common cancers in males**: Lung, oral, colorectal, stomach, esophageal
- **Common cancers in females**: Breast, cervical, ovarian, colorectal, lung

**Regional Variations**:
- **Delhi**: Highest incidence rates
- **Northeast**: High esophageal and gall bladder cancer
- **South**: High oral and breast cancer
- **Urban vs Rural**: Higher incidence in urban areas

#### Common Cancers

**Oral Cancer**:
- **Burden**: 2nd most common cancer in India
- **Risk factors**: Tobacco chewing, smoking, alcohol, HPV
- **Prevention**: Tobacco control, early detection
- **Screening**: Visual examination of oral cavity

**Breast Cancer**:
- **Burden**: Most common cancer in Indian women
- **Incidence**: 162,468 new cases annually (2020)
- **Age**: Median age 50-55 years
- **Risk factors**: Late marriage, late first child, less breastfeeding, obesity

**Cervical Cancer**:
- **Burden**: 2nd most common cancer in Indian women
- **Declining trend**: Vaccination and screening programs
- **Risk factors**: Early marriage, multiple pregnancies, HPV infection
- **Prevention**: HPV vaccination, screening with VIA/VILI

**Lung Cancer**:
- **Burden**: Most common cancer in Indian males
- **Incidence rising**: Due to tobacco smoking
- **Risk factors**: Tobacco smoking, air pollution, occupational exposure
- **Prognosis**: Poor due to late detection

#### Risk Factors and Prevention

**Tobacco-Related Cancers**:
- **Risk factors**: Tobacco smoking, smokeless tobacco, passive smoking
- **Prevention**: Tobacco control policies, taxation, advertising ban

**Infectious Agent-Related Cancers**:
- **HPV**: Cervical, anal, oropharyngeal cancers
- **Hepatitis B & C**: Hepatocellular carcinoma
- **H. pylori**: Gastric cancer
- **Prevention**: Vaccination, treatment of infections

**Lifestyle-Related Cancers**:
- **Diet**: Processed meat, low fiber, high fat
- **Physical inactivity**: Breast, colorectal cancer risk
- **Alcohol**: Multiple cancer types
- **Obesity**: Multiple cancer types

**Occupational Cancers**:
- **Asbestos**: Mesothelioma, lung cancer
- **Benzene**: Leukemia
- **Arsenic**: Skin, lung, bladder cancer
- **Prevention**: Workplace safety, exposure limits

#### Cancer Registry Systems

**National Cancer Registry Programme (NCRP)**:
- **Started**: 1981
- **Network**: 28 population-based cancer registries
- **Coverage**: 10% of population
- **Data collection**: Population-based and hospital-based registries

**Types of Registries**:
1. **Population-Based Cancer Registries (PBCR)**:
   - Collect data on all cancer cases in defined population
   - Incidence and mortality rates
   - Trend analysis

2. **Hospital-Based Cancer Registries (HBCR)**:
   - Collect data on cancer patients in specific hospitals
   - Treatment patterns and outcomes
   - Clinical research

**Cancer Atlas**:
- Maps cancer incidence across India
- Identifies high-risk areas
- Guides prevention strategies

#### Screening Programs (NCRP Recommendations)

**Screening Recommendations**:

**Breast Cancer**:
- **Method**: Clinical breast examination (CBE)
- **Age**: 30-65 years
- **Frequency**: Every 3 years
- **Population**: All women

**Cervical Cancer**:
- **Method**: Visual inspection with acetic acid (VIA)
- **Age**: 30-65 years
- **Frequency**: Every 5 years
- **Population**: All women

**Oral Cancer**:
- **Method**: Visual examination of oral cavity
- **Age**: 30+ years
- **Frequency**: Every 3 years
- **Population**: High-risk individuals (tobacco users)

**Colorectal Cancer**:
- **Method**: Fecal occult blood test (FOBT)
- **Age**: 50-75 years
- **Frequency**: Annually
- **Population**: Average risk individuals

**National Cancer Screening Program**:
- **Launched**: 2017 as part of NCD control
- **Coverage**: District level screening
- **Focus**: Common cancers in women (breast, cervical)
- **Integration**: With NPCDCS program

## Chapter 30: Mental Health

### Section A: Mental Health Concepts

#### Definition and WHO Framework

**WHO Definition (2021)**:
"Mental health is a state of well-being in which an individual realizes his or her own abilities, can cope with the normal stresses of life, can work productively and is able to make a contribution to his or her community."

**WHO Framework (2013-2030)**:
1. **Mental health promotion**: Build positive mental health
2. **Mental disorder prevention**: Prevent mental disorders
3. **Treatment and care**: Evidence-based interventions
4. **Recovery**: Community integration and social inclusion

**Mental Health Continuum**:
- **Mental well-being**: Positive mental health
- **Mental distress**: Stress, anxiety (temporary)
- **Mental disorder**: Clinical diagnosis required
- **Severe mental illness**: Chronic, disabling conditions

#### Common Mental Disorders

**Depression**:
- **Global prevalence**: 5% of adults
- **India**: 3-5% point prevalence
- **Symptoms**: Depressed mood, anhedonia, sleep/appetite changes, guilt, suicidal thoughts
- **Impact**: 2nd leading cause of disability

**Anxiety Disorders**:
- **Types**: GAD, panic disorder, social anxiety, specific phobias
- **Global prevalence**: 3.8% of population
- **India**: 3-4% prevalence
- **Symptoms**: Excessive worry, restlessness, muscle tension, sleep disturbance

**Suicide**:
- **Global**: 703,000 deaths annually
- **India**: 163,000 deaths annually (2019)
- **Age-standardized rate**: 14.3 per 100,000
- **Gender ratio**: 3:1 male to female ratio

**Substance Use Disorders**:
- **Alcohol**: 43% of males, 4% of females use alcohol regularly
- **Tobacco**: 267 million tobacco users (2019)
- **Drugs**: Growing problem, especially among youth

#### Stigma and Discrimination

**Types of Stigma**:
1. **Public stigma**: Negative attitudes from society
2. **Self-stigma**: Internalization by affected individuals
3. **Institutional stigma**: Discriminatory policies and practices

**Impact of Stigma**:
- **Delayed help-seeking**: Stigma prevents early treatment
- **Social isolation**: Exclusion from family and community
- **Reduced adherence**: Stoppage of treatment
- **Employment**: Discrimination in workplace

**Anti-Stigma Strategies**:
1. **Education**: Correct misconceptions about mental illness
2. **Contact**: Direct interaction with people with mental illness
3. **Protest**: Challenging discriminatory practices
4. **Advocacy**: Supporting policy changes

#### Mental Health in India

**Burden of Disease**:
- **Years of life lost**: 2,181 per 100,000 population
- **Disability-adjusted life years**: 2,444 per 100,000
- **Contribution to total DALYs**: 13% of all DALYs
- **Unipolar depression**: 2nd leading cause of years lived with disability

**Treatment Gap**:
- **Depression**: 85% treatment gap
- **Anxiety disorders**: 90% treatment gap
- **Psychosis**: 75% treatment gap
- **Epilepsy**: 50% treatment gap

**Barriers to Treatment**:
1. **Economic**: Poverty, lack of insurance
2. **Geographic**: Rural-urban disparities
3. **Cultural**: Stigma, cultural beliefs
4. **Health system**: Shortage of providers, infrastructure

### Section B: Substance Abuse

#### Alcohol Use Disorders

**Global Burden**:
- **3 million deaths** annually
- **5.1% of disease burden** (DALYs)
- **Harmful use**: 2.3 billion people

**Indian Statistics**:
- **Current alcohol use**: 14.6% of males, 2.9% of females
- **Alcohol dependence**: 2.7% of males, 0.3% of females
- **Per capita consumption**: 4.3 liters of pure alcohol per year
- **Alcohol-attributable deaths**: 3.7% of all deaths

**Alcohol Use in India**:
- **Patterns**: Binge drinking, weekend patterns
- **Types**: Country liquor, IMFL, beer
- **Age**: Lowering age of initiation
- **Gender**: Rapidly increasing among women

**Alcohol-Related Problems**:
- **Health**: Liver disease, cardiovascular disease, cancer, mental disorders
- **Social**: Domestic violence, child abuse, road traffic accidents
- **Economic**: Productivity loss, healthcare costs

#### Tobacco Use and Cessation

**Global Tobacco Epidemic**:
- **1.1 billion tobacco users**: 80% in low/middle-income countries
- **Tobacco deaths**: 8 million annually (7 million smokers, 1.2 million secondhand)
- **Economic burden**: $1.4 trillion annually

**Indian Tobacco Use**:
- **Overall prevalence**: 267 million (28.6% of adults)
- **Smokers**: 106 million (14% of adults)
- **Smokeless tobacco**: 200 million users (21.4% of adults)
- **Dual users**: Both smoking and smokeless tobacco

**Tobacco Control Measures (MPOWER)**:
- **M**onitor tobacco use
- **P**rotect from secondhand smoke
- **O**ffer help to quit
- **W**arn about dangers
- **E**nforce advertising bans
- **R**aise taxes on tobacco

**Cessation Strategies**:
1. **Brief intervention**: 5A's approach (Ask, Advise, Assess, Assist, Arrange)
2. **Pharmacotherapy**: NRT, bupropion, varenicline
3. **Behavioral counseling**: Individual and group therapy
4. **Community programs**: Mass media campaigns

#### Drug Abuse and Addiction

**Global Drug Use**:
- **275 million people** used drugs in 2020
- **36 million people** with drug use disorders
- **Cannabis**: Most used drug (200 million users)
- **Opioids**: Most harmful, 13.5 million users

**Drug Situation in India**:
- **Cannabis**: 3.3 million users
- **Opioids**: 8.5 million users (2.1% of population)
- **Prescription drugs**: Growing concern (tranquilizers, sedatives)
- **Synthetic drugs**: Emerging threat

**Risk Factors for Drug Abuse**:
1. **Individual**: Genetic vulnerability, mental illness, trauma
2. **Family**: Family history, family conflict, poor supervision
3. **Peer**: Peer pressure, drug-using peers
4. **School**: Academic failure, school dropout
5. **Community**: Poverty, availability, social norms

#### Public Health Approaches

**Prevention Strategies**:
1. **Universal prevention**: Population-wide interventions
2. **Selective prevention**: Targeted high-risk groups
3. **Indicated prevention**: Early intervention for at-risk individuals

**Treatment Approaches**:
1. **Detoxification**: Medical management of withdrawal
2. **Psychosocial interventions**: Counseling, CBT, family therapy
3. **Pharmacotherapy**: For opioid dependence (methadone, buprenorphine)
4. **Recovery support**: Rehabilitation, vocational training

**Policy Framework**:
- **Drug demand reduction**: Prevention, treatment, rehabilitation
- **Drug supply reduction**: Law enforcement, international cooperation
- **Harm reduction**: Needle exchange, opioid substitution therapy

- **Critical period**: First 2-5 years after onset

**Child and Adolescent Mental Health**:
- **Prevention**: Parent education, school programs

- **Intervention**: Family therapy, school-based interventions

**Suicide Prevention**:
- **Risk factors**: Mental illness, substance abuse, stress
- **Warning signs**: Talking about death, giving away possessions, sudden mood changes

- **Suicide rate**: 12.3 per 100,000 population (2020)
- **Rural urban**: Higher rates in rural areas
- **Age**: Peak in 20-24 years

**Prevention Strategies**:
1. **Gatekeeper training**: Train community leaders to identify warning signs

- **Right to healthcare**: Mental healthcare as fundamental right

**Key Provisions**:
1. **Equality**: Non-discrimination in healthcare
2. **Access**: Affordable, accessible, quality care
3. **Privacy**: Confidentiality of health information
4. **Community care**: Least restrictive environment

**Implementation Challenges**:

- **Training needs**: Capacity building of healthcare providers
- **Awareness**: Public education about rights
- **Integration**: Coordination between sectors

## Chapter 31: Accidents and Injuries

### Section A: Road Traffic Accidents

#### Epidemiology of Road Traffic Injuries

**Global Burden**:
- **1.35 million deaths** annually (2018)
- **Leading cause of death**: Ages 5-29 years
- **20-50 million injuries**: Non-fatal injuries annually
- **Economic cost**: $1.8 trillion annually (1-3% of GDP)

**Indian Statistics**:
- **Deaths**: 154,000 deaths annually (2019)
- **Injuries**: 300,000+ injured annually
- **Death rate**: 16.6 per 100,000 population
- **Under-reporting**: Actual deaths may be 2-3 times higher

**Key Characteristics**:
- **Victims**: 71% are aged 18-60 years (productive age)
- **Gender**: Males account for 85% of deaths
- **Time pattern**: Peak during festival seasons
- **Location**: 60% deaths occur on national highways

#### Risk Factors and Prevention

**Human Factors**:
1. **Speed**: Major factor in severity of crashes
2. **Alcohol**: 20-30% of road traffic deaths alcohol-related
3. **Distraction**: Mobile phone use, other distractions
4. **Non-use of helmets**: 60% of two-wheeler deaths
5. **Non-use of seatbelts**: Increases fatality risk by 50%

**Vehicle Factors**:
- **Roadworthiness**: Poor maintenance, defective brakes
- **Overloading**: Especially commercial vehicles
- **Unsafe vehicles**: Poor visibility, inadequate brakes
- **Two-wheelers**: 25% of vehicles, 45% of deaths

**Environmental Factors**:
- **Road conditions**: Poor infrastructure, potholes
- **Weather**: Rain, fog affecting visibility
- **Lighting**: Poor street lighting
- **Urban design**: Lack of pedestrian facilities

#### Haddon's Matrix Application

**Haddon's Matrix**:
|            | Human Factor | Vehicle Factor | Environmental Factor |
|------------|-------------|---------------|---------------------|
| Pre-crash  | Education, training | Vehicle safety, maintenance | Road design, signage |
| Crash      | Use of restraints | Crash worthiness | Safety barriers |
| Post-crash | First aid, rescue | Fire prevention | Emergency care |

**Prevention Strategies Using Haddon's Matrix**:
1. **Pre-crash phase**: Education, legislation, vehicle safety
2. **Crash phase**: Safety devices (airbags, seatbelts)
3. **Post-crash phase**: Emergency care, rehabilitation

#### Road Safety Measures

**Engineering Measures**:
1. **Infrastructure**: Road design, traffic calming
2. **Vehicle safety**: Crash worthiness standards
3. **Technology**: ITS (Intelligent Transport Systems)
4. **Maintenance**: Regular road and vehicle maintenance

**Education Measures**:
1. **Public awareness**: Road safety campaigns
2. **Driver training**: Formal and informal education
3. **School programs**: Child pedestrian education
4. **Media campaigns**: Mass media interventions

**Enforcement Measures**:
1. **Speed limits**: 30-50 km/h in urban areas
2. **Drink-driving**: Legal limit 0.03% BAC
3. **Helmet laws**: Compulsory for two-wheeler riders
4. **Seatbelt laws**: Front and rear seatbelt use

**Emergency Trauma Care**:
- **Golden hour**: First 60 minutes most critical
- **Pre-hospital care**: Ambulance services, trained paramedics
- **Hospital care**: Trauma centers, specialized care
- **Rehabilitation**: Long-term care and rehabilitation

### Section B: Other Injuries

#### Occupational Injuries

**Global Burden**:
- **340 million workplace accidents** annually
- **2.3 million work-related deaths** annually
- **340 million lost workdays** annually
- **Economic impact**: 4% of global GDP

**Indian Occupational Safety**:
- **Factories Act 1948**: Basic safety standards
- **Construction workers**: High-risk occupation
- **Agricultural injuries**: Most workers affected
- **Informal sector**: 93% of workforce, minimal protection

**Major Occupational Risks**:
1. **Physical hazards**: Noise, vibration, extreme temperatures
2. **Chemical hazards**: Dust, gases, chemicals
3. **Biological hazards**: Bacteria, viruses, fungi
4. **Ergonomic hazards**: Repetitive motion, poor posture
5. **Psychosocial hazards**: Stress, workplace violence

**Prevention Strategies**:
1. **Engineering controls**: Eliminate hazards at source
2. **Administrative controls**: Work organization, training
3. **Personal protective equipment**: Last line of defense
4. **Surveillance**: Health and safety monitoring

#### Home Injuries

**Global Burden**:
- **4.5 million deaths** annually (all ages)
- **Higher in children**: Falls, burns, drowning
- **Higher in elderly**: Falls, poisoning
- **Economic impact**: Significant healthcare costs

**Indian Home Safety**:

### Section C: Injury Prevention

#### Haddon Matrix and Prevention Strategies

**Traditional Haddon Matrix**:
| Phase | Human | Agent/Vehicle | Environment |
|-------|-------|---------------|-------------|
| Pre-event | Knowledge, attitudes, training | Equipment design, maintenance | Physical environment design |
| Event | Size, age, health | Speed, weight, protection | Surfaces, roadside hazards |
| Post-event | First aid training, survival skills | Post-crash fuel ignition | Emergency care access |

**Injury Prevention Hierarchy**:
1. **Most effective**: Eliminate the hazard
2. **Substitute**: Replace with safer alternative
3. **Isolate**: Contain the hazard
4. **Restructure**: Engineer safety features
5. **Train**: Educate users
6. **Regulate**: Enforce safe practices

**Examples of Haddon Matrix Application**:

**Road Traffic Injuries**:
- **Pre-event**: Speed enforcement, driver training
- **Event**: Vehicle crash worthiness, seatbelts
- **Post-event**: Emergency medical services

**Burn Injuries**:
- **Pre-event**: Electrical safety standards, fire safety education
- **Event**: Automatic shutoffs, flame-resistant clothing
- **Post-event**: Burn care units, rehabilitation

#### Environmental Modifications

**Safer Design Principles**:
1. **Passive protection**: No user action required
2. **Active protection**: User action required
3. **Education**: Knowledge and skills development
4. **Enforcement**: Legal and regulatory measures

**Examples of Environmental Modifications**:

**Road Safety**:
- **Physical infrastructure**: Median barriers, crash cushions
- **Traffic calming**: Speed bumps, chicanes, roundabouts
- **Lighting**: Adequate street lighting, reflective markings

**Home Safety**:
- **Stair safety**: Handrails, anti-slip surfaces
- **Kitchen safety**: Gas leak detectors, automatic shutoffs

- **Electrical safety**: Grounded outlets, circuit breakers

### Clinical Case Scenarios

#### Case 1: Coronary Artery Disease
**Scenario**: 55-year-old male, executive, presents with chest pain on exertion
**Risk Factors Present**: 
- Sedentary lifestyle, stress, hypertension (undetected), smoking (recently quit)
- Family history of heart disease
**Prevention Strategy**: 
- Primary prevention with risk factor modification
- Screening for diabetes, lipids
- Lifestyle counseling
**Discussion Points**: Primordial vs primary prevention, Framingham risk assessment

#### Case 2: Diabetes Management
**Scenario**: 45-year-old female, teacher, diagnosed with type 2 diabetes
**Current Status**: 
- HbA1c 8.5%, BP 140/90 mmHg, BMI 32 kg/m²
- Family history of diabetes
**Management Plan**:
- Lifestyle modification (diet, exercise)
- Metformin initiation
- BP control (target <140/90 mmHg)
- Regular monitoring and complication screening
**Discussion Points**: ADA vs WHO criteria, IDRS scoring, microvascular complications

#### Case 3: Mental Health Crisis
**Scenario**: 28-year-old male, recent job loss, presenting with 2 weeks of low mood, sleep disturbance, suicidal thoughts
**Assessment**:
- Depression with suicidal ideation
- No previous psychiatric history
- Social stressors (job loss, financial)
**Intervention**:
- Suicide risk assessment
- Brief intervention and crisis support
- Referral for counseling/therapy
- Follow-up and monitoring
**Discussion Points**: Stigma, treatment gap, NMHP components

**Scenario**: 22-year-old motorcyclist, helmet-less, collision with car at 60 km/h speed

**Prevention Analysis**:
- Pre-crash: Helmet non-use, speed violation

- Post-crash: EMS response, trauma care
**Prevention Opportunities**:
- Enforcement of helmet laws

- EMS system improvement
