# Part IV: Maternal and Child Health

## Chapter 11: Maternal Health

### Section A: Reproductive Health

#### Definition and Concepts
**Reproductive Health** is a state of complete physical, mental and social well-being and not merely the absence of disease or infirmity, in all matters relating to the reproductive system and to its functions and processes.

**Reproductive Rights** are based on the recognition of the basic right of all couples and individuals to decide freely and responsibly on the number, spacing and timing of their children and to have the information and means to do so.

**Key Components:**
- Maternal and newborn care
- Family planning services
- Prevention and management of reproductive tract infections and STDs
- Elimination of harmful practices (female genital mutilation, son preference)
- Promotion of sexual health

#### Maternal Mortality and Morbidity

**Maternal Mortality Ratio (MMR):**
- **Definition:** Number of maternal deaths per 100,000 live births
- **Calculation:** MMR = (Maternal deaths ÷ Live births) × 100,000
- **NFHS-5 Data:** India MMR = 97 per 100,000 live births (2020)
- **Global Average:** 211 per 100,000 live births
- **Target 3.1 of SDGs:** Reduce global MMR to less than 70 per 100,000 live births

**Maternal Mortality Causes (Three Delays Model):**
1. **Delay in deciding to seek care**
2. **Delay in reaching care**
3. **Delay in receiving adequate care**

**Major Causes of Maternal Deaths:**
1. **Hemorrhage (27%)**
2. **Hypertensive disorders (14%)**
3. **Sepsis (11%)**
4. **Pulmonary embolism (3%)**
5. **Amniotic fluid embolism (2%)**
6. **Cardiac disease (4%)**
7. **Other causes (39%)**

#### Risk Factors and Assessment

**High-Risk Pregnancy Identification:**

**Maternal Risk Factors:**
- Age < 17 years or > 35 years
- Parity 0 or ≥ 4
- Height < 145 cm
- Weight < 45 kg or > 80 kg
- Previous obstetric complications
- Medical conditions (diabetes, hypertension, heart disease)
- Substance abuse

**Obstetric Risk Factors:**
- Antepartum hemorrhage
- Pre-eclampsia/eclampsia
- Multiple pregnancy
- Fetal growth restriction
- Malpresentation
- Post-term pregnancy
- Previous cesarean section

**Social Risk Factors:**
- Low socioeconomic status
- Poor education
- Domestic violence
- Unmarried mother
- Lack of family support

### Section B: Antenatal Care

#### WHO Recommended ANC Package
**Essential Components of ANC:**
1. **Risk assessment**
2. **Monitoring and management**
3. **Health education and promotion**
4. **Counseling and support**

**Focused Antenatal Care (FANC):**
- **Minimum 4 visits** for uncomplicated pregnancy
- **8 contacts model:** 1 in 1st trimester, 1 in 2nd trimester, 2 in 3rd trimester

#### ANC Schedule and Components

**First Visit (8-12 weeks):**
- **Complete history and examination**
- **Weight, height, BMI calculation**
- **Blood pressure measurement**
- **Laboratory investigations:**
  - Hemoglobin estimation
  - Blood grouping and Rh typing
  - Urine analysis
  - Blood sugar (fasting/PP)
  - VDRL/RPR test
  - HIV testing (with consent)
  - HBsAg test
- **Tetanus toxoid immunization (if not protected)**
- **Iron and folic acid supplementation**

**Subsequent Visits (16-20 weeks, 24-26 weeks, 32-34 weeks, 36-38 weeks):**
- **Weight gain monitoring**
- **Blood pressure measurement**
- **Fundal height measurement**
- **Fetal heart sounds**
- **Fetal movements (after 28 weeks)**
- **Urine testing for protein and glucose**
- **Anemia screening**

#### Danger Signs Recognition

**First Trimester Danger Signs:**
- Bleeding per vaginum
- Severe nausea and vomiting
- Abdominal pain
- Fever with vaginal discharge
- Severe headache with visual disturbances

**Second Trimester Danger Signs:**
- Bleeding per vaginum
- Severe headache
- Visual disturbances
- Epigastric pain
- Decreased fetal movements
- Fluid leak per vaginum

**Third Trimester Danger Signs:**
- Bleeding per vaginum
- Severe headache
- Visual disturbances
- Epigastric pain
- Reduced fetal movements
- Abdominal pain with rigidity
- Convulsions
- Severe swelling of face and hands

#### Birth Preparedness
**Birth Preparedness Components:**
- **Identify skilled birth attendant**
- **Arrange transport for emergency**
- **Save money for emergency**
- **Identify blood donor**
- **Arrange companion for delivery**
- **Prepare clean delivery kit**
- **Identify nearest health facility**

#### Tetanus Toxoid Immunization
**Schedule:**
- **TT1:** At first contact or as early as possible
- **TT2:** 4 weeks after TT1
- **TT3:** 6 months after TT2 or during next pregnancy
- **TT4:** 1 year after TT3 or during next pregnancy
- **TT5:** 1 year after TT4 or during next pregnancy

**Protection Duration:**
- **TT1 + TT2:** 3 years protection
- **TT3 + TT4:** 5 years protection
- **TT5:** Full protection for all future pregnancies

#### Iron and Folic Acid Supplementation
**Dosage:**
- **Iron:** 60 mg elemental iron daily
- **Folic acid:** 400 μg daily
- **Duration:** Throughout pregnancy and 3 months postpartum

**Benefits:**
- Reduces maternal anemia by 70%
- Reduces preterm births by 18%
- Reduces low birth weight by 16%

### Section C: Intranatal Care

#### Clean Delivery Practices
**Essential Elements:**
1. **Clean hands**
2. **Clean perineum**
3. **Clean delivery surface**
4. **Clean cord cutting**
5. **Clean cord tying**
6. **Eye care for newborn**
7. **Thermal care for newborn**

**Hand Washing Technique:**
1. Wet hands with clean water
2. Apply soap and lather well
3. Scrub palms, backs of hands, between fingers, and under nails
4. Rinse thoroughly with clean water
5. Dry with clean towel or air dry

#### Skilled Birth Attendance
**Definition:** Trained health professional with competencies to manage normal pregnancy and childbirth, and identify and manage complications or refer for complications.

**Competencies Required:**
- **Antenatal care skills**
- **Normal delivery management**
- **Emergency obstetric care**
- **Newborn care**
- **Communication skills**
- **Documentation and reporting**

#### Emergency Obstetric Care (EmOC)
**Basic EmOC Functions:**
1. **Parenteral antibiotics**
2. **Parenteral uterotonics**
3. **Parenteral anticonvulsants**
4. **Manual removal of placenta**
5. **Removal of retained products**
6. **Assisted vaginal delivery**
7. **Neonatal resuscitation**

**Comprehensive EmOC Functions:**
1. **All basic functions**
2. **Surgery (cesarean section)**
3. **Blood transfusion**

#### Partograph Usage
**Purpose:** Monitor labor progress and detect complications early.

**Components:**
- **Fetal condition:** FHR, molding of skull bones, amniotic fluid
- **Maternal condition:** Pulse, BP, temperature, urine output
- **Labor progress:** Cervical dilation, descent of head
- **Drugs and fluids administered**
- **Time elapsed**

**Alert Line:** Represents normal labor progress
**Action Line:** 4 hours to the right of alert line

#### Active Management of Third Stage (AMTSL)
**Steps:**
1. **Uterotonic drug:** Oxytocin 10 IU IM/IV immediately after delivery
2. **Controlled cord traction**
3. **Uterine massage**

**Benefits:**
- Reduces postpartum hemorrhage by 60-70%
- Reduces blood loss by 250-300 ml
- Reduces retained placenta

### Section D: Postnatal Care

#### Postpartum Care Protocols
**First 24 hours:**
- Monitor vital signs
- Check fundal height and tone
- Monitor bleeding
- Assess urine output
- Breastfeeding support
- Pain management

**First week:**
- Continue monitoring
- Perineal care
- Breast care
- Contraception counseling
- Nutritional counseling

**6 weeks postpartum:**
- Complete examination
- Contraceptive method provision
- Health education reinforcement

#### Breastfeeding Support
**Optimal Breastfeeding Practices:**
- **Early initiation:** Within 1 hour of birth
- **Exclusive breastfeeding:** First 6 months
- **Continued breastfeeding:** Up to 2 years with complementary foods

**Benefits:**
- **For infant:** Reduces infections, improves cognitive development
- **For mother:** Reduces postpartum bleeding, aids contraception

**Breastfeeding Positions:**
- **Cradle hold**
- **Cross-cradle**
- **Football hold**
- **Side-lying**
- **Biological nurturing**

#### Postpartum Complications
**Major Complications:**
1. **Postpartum hemorrhage (PPH)**
2. **Puerperal sepsis**
3. **Venous thromboembolism**
4. **Postpartum cardiomyopathy**
5. **Mental health disorders**

**PPH Management (4 Ts):**
- **Tone:** Uterine atony (80% cases)
- **Trauma:** Lacerations, hematoma
- **Tissue:** Retained placenta
- **Thrombin:** Coagulation disorders

#### Family Planning Counseling
**Postpartum Contraception Options:**
- **Barrier methods:** Condoms, diaphragms
- **Hormonal methods:** Progestin-only pills, implants
- **Intrauterine devices:** Copper IUDs
- **Permanent methods:** Sterilization

**Lactational Amenorrhea Method (LAM):**
- **Conditions:** <6 months postpartum, exclusive breastfeeding, amenorrhea
- **Effectiveness:** 98% if followed correctly

### Section B: Newborn Care

#### Essential Newborn Care
**Key Elements:**
1. **Thermal care**
2. **Cleanliness**
3. **Early and exclusive breastfeeding**
4. **Resuscitation**
5. **Immunization**
6. **Care of the small baby**

**Thermal Care Principles:**
- **Dry immediately after birth**
- **Skin-to-skin contact (Kangaroo Mother Care)**
- **Keep warm with blankets**
- **Avoid hypothermia (temperature <36.5°C)**

#### Birth Asphyxia Management
**Definition:** Failure to initiate spontaneous breathing within 1 minute of birth.

**Risk Factors:**
- **Maternal:** Severe anemia, hypertension, diabetes
- **Obstetric:** Prolonged labor, fetal distress, cord prolapse
- **Fetal:** Prematurity, multiple pregnancy, IUGR

**Management Steps:**
1. **Immediate assessment**
2. **Dry and stimulate**
3. **Clear airway**
4. **Ventilate with bag and mask**
5. **Chest compressions if needed**
6. **Adrenaline if no response**

#### Newborn Resuscitation
**AAP/AHA Neonatal Resuscitation Algorithm:**
1. **Initial steps:** Warm, dry, stimulate, position
2. **PPV:** If not breathing or gasping
3. **Chest compressions:** If HR <60 after 30 seconds PPV
4. **Medications:** Adrenaline if HR <60 after adequate ventilation and chest compressions

**Equipment Required:**
- **Radiant warmer**
- **Suction device**
- **Bag and mask**
- **Oxygen source**
- **Endotracheal tubes**
- **Stethoscope**

#### Neonatal Hypothermia Prevention
**WHO Classification:**
- **Cold stress:** 36.0-36.4°C
- **Moderate hypothermia:** 32.0-35.9°C
- **Severe hypothermia:** <32.0°C

**Prevention Methods:**
- **Immediate drying**
- **Skin-to-skin contact**
- **Warm blankets**
- **Heated room (25-28°C)**
- **Warm delivery practices**

#### Kangaroo Mother Care (KMC)
**Definition:** Care of preterm/low birth weight babies through continuous skin-to-skin contact between mother and baby.

**Benefits:**
- **Thermal regulation**
- **Improved breastfeeding**
- **Reduced infections**
- **Better weight gain**
- **Enhanced mother-infant bonding**
- **Reduced mortality**

**Implementation:**
- **Continuous 24/7 skin-to-skin contact**
- **Exclusive breastfeeding**
- **Early discharge**
- **Support to mother**
- **Follow-up care**

### Section C: Common Childhood Problems

#### Diarrheal Diseases Management
**Classification by Duration:**
- **Acute:** <14 days
- **Persistent:** >14 days
- **Chronic:** >30 days

**WHO Classification of Dehydration:**
- **No dehydration**
- **Some dehydration**
- **Severe dehydration**

**Treatment Based on Dehydration Status:**

**No Dehydration:**
- **Treatment:** ORS at home
- **Amount:** 10-20 ml/kg after each loose stool
- **Continue normal feeding**

**Some Dehydration:**
- **Treatment:** ORS in health facility
- **Amount:** 75 ml/kg over 4 hours
- **Reassess every 2 hours**
- **Continue feeding**

**Severe Dehydration:**
- **Treatment:** IV fluids immediately
- **Ringer's Lactate or Normal Saline**
- **100 ml/kg over 3-6 hours**
- **Reassess every 15-30 minutes**

#### Acute Respiratory Infections
**WHO Classification:**

**No Pneumonia:**
- **Cough or cold**
- **Treatment:** Home care, no antibiotics**
- **Danger signs:** Severe malnutrition, lethargy, poor feeding

**Pneumonia:**
- **Fast breathing present**
- **Treatment:** Oral antibiotics (amoxicillin)
- **Fast breathing rates:** >60/min (0-2 months), >50/min (2-12 months), >40/min (1-5 years)

**Severe Pneumonia:**
- **Chest indrawing**
- **Treatment:** Hospital admission, IV antibiotics
- **Additional signs:** Stridor, lethargy, poor feeding

**Very Severe Disease:**
- **Unable to drink, convulsions, lethargy**
- **Treatment:** Emergency referral
- **Start treatment before referral**

#### Malnutrition in Children
**Classification (IAP Guidelines):**

**Underweight:**
- **Moderate:** Weight for age <3rd percentile but ≥1st percentile
- **Severe:** Weight for age <1st percentile

**Stunting:**
- **Moderate:** Height for age <3rd percentile but ≥1st percentile
- **Severe:** Height for age <1st percentile

**Wasting:**
- **Moderate:** Weight for height <3rd percentile but ≥1st percentile
- **Severe:** Weight for height <1st percentile

**Severe Acute Malnutrition (SAM):**
- **Weight for height <3rd percentile OR**
- **Mid-arm circumference <11.5 cm (6-59 months) OR**
- **Bilateral pedal edema**

**Management of SAM:**
- **Outpatient management:** If no complications and appetite present
- **Inpatient management:** If complications or poor appetite
- **Therapeutic foods:** RUTF (Ready-to-Use Therapeutic Food)

#### Micronutrient Deficiencies
**Vitamin A Deficiency:**
- **Causes:** Inadequate intake, malabsorption
- **Manifestations:** Night blindness, Bitot's spots, corneal xerosis, blindness
- **Prevention:** High-dose vitamin A supplementation (100,000 IU every 6 months from 6 months to 5 years)

**Iron Deficiency Anemia:**
- **Causes:** Inadequate intake, increased demand, parasitic infections
- **Manifestations:** Pallor, fatigue, reduced immunity
- **Prevention:** Iron supplementation, deworming

**Iodine Deficiency Disorders:**
- **Causes:** Low iodine in soil/water
- **Manifestations:** Goiter, mental retardation, cretinism, deafness
- **Prevention:** Universal salt iodization (15-20 ppm iodine)

4. **Poisoning**
5. **Falls**

**Prevention Strategies:**
- **Environmental safety measures**

## Chapter 13: Geriatric Health

### Section A: Aging Process

#### Biological vs Chronological Aging
**Chronological Aging:**
- **Definition:** Number of years since birth
- **Measure:** Calendar age
- **Population aging:** Countries with >7% population aged ≥65 years

**Biological Aging:**
- **Definition:** Physiological changes in body systems
- **Individual variation:** Different rates despite same chronological age
- **Assessment:** Functional capacity, biomarkers

**Geriatric Age Groups:**
- **Young old:** 65-74 years
- **Middle old:** 75-84 years
- **Oldest old:** ≥85 years

#### Age-Related Changes

**Physiological Changes:**

**Cardiovascular System:**
- **Arterial stiffening**
- **Reduced cardiac output**
- **Increased systolic BP**
- **Reduced heart rate variability**

**Respiratory System:**
- **Reduced lung capacity**
- **Decreased gas exchange**
- **Reduced cough reflex**
- **Increased respiratory infections**

**Musculoskeletal System:**
- **Bone density loss (osteoporosis)**
- **Muscle mass reduction (sarcopenia)**
- **Joint cartilage degeneration**
- **Reduced flexibility**

**Nervous System:**
- **Cognitive processing speed reduction**
- **Memory changes**
- **Sensory loss (vision, hearing)**
- **Reduced reflexes**

**Immune System:**
- **Immunosenescence**
- **Reduced vaccine response**
- **Increased infection susceptibility**

#### Theories of Aging
**Biological Theories:**

**Programmed Aging:**
- **Genetic clock theory**
- **Cellular senescence**
- **Telomere shortening**

**Error/Cumulative Damage Theory:**
- **Free radical theory**
- **Protein cross-linking theory**
- **DNA damage accumulation**

**Hormonal Theories:**
- **Endocrine dysfunction**
- **Growth hormone deficiency**
- **Melatonin changes**

**Immunological Theories:**
- **Immune system aging**
- **Autoimmunity increase**
- **Inflammation (inflammaging)**

### Section C: Common Geriatric Problems

#### Cardiovascular Diseases
**Hypertension in Elderly:**
- **Definition:** BP ≥130/80 mmHg (updated criteria)
- **Special considerations:** Orthostatic hypotension, white coat hypertension
- **Treatment:** Lifestyle modification, appropriate antihypertensive therapy

**Coronary Artery Disease:**
- **Risk factors:** Age, hypertension, diabetes, smoking
- **Presentation:** Atypical symptoms in elderly
- **Management:** Medical therapy, revascularization when appropriate

**Heart Failure:**
- **Prevalence:** 6-10% in ≥65 years
- **Causes:** Hypertension, coronary artery disease, valvular disease
- **Management:** ACE inhibitors, beta-blockers, diuretics

#### Musculoskeletal Disorders
**Osteoporosis:**
- **Definition:** Bone mineral density ≤2.5 SD below young adult mean
- **Risk factors:** Postmenopausal status, glucocorticoids, family history
- **Prevention:** Calcium (1200 mg/day), vitamin D (800 IU/day)
- **Treatment:** Bisphosphonates, hormone therapy

**Osteoarthritis:**
- **Most common joint disease in elderly**
- **Common sites:** Knee, hip, hand, spine
- **Management:** Exercise, weight management, analgesics, joint injections

**Sarcopenia:**
- **Definition:** Age-related loss of muscle mass and function
- **Prevalence:** Increases with age, affects 10% at age 50, 30% at age 80
- **Prevention:** Resistance exercise, adequate protein intake

#### Cognitive Impairment
**Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI):**
- **Definition:** Cognitive decline greater than expected for age but no significant functional impairment
- **Types:** Amnestic, non-amnestic
- **Progression:** 10-15% annually progress to dementia

**Dementia:**
- **Definition:** Progressive decline in multiple cognitive domains affecting daily function
- **Types:** Alzheimer's disease (60-70%), vascular dementia, Lewy body dementia
- **Staging:** Mild, moderate, severe

**Alzheimer's Disease:**
- **Most common cause of dementia**
- **Pathophysiology:** Amyloid plaques, neurofibrillary tangles
- **Stages:** Early (memory), middle (behavioral), late (severe cognitive decline)

**Management Principles:**
- **Early diagnosis and intervention**
- **Cognitive stimulation therapy**
- **Medication:** Cholinesterase inhibitors, memantine
- **Safety measures and caregiver support**

#### Vision and Hearing Problems
**Vision Problems:**
- **Presbyopia:** Age-related loss of accommodation (40+ years)
- **Cataracts:** Leading cause of blindness in elderly
- **Glaucoma:** Second leading cause of blindness
- **Age-related macular degeneration:** Leading cause of irreversible blindness

**Hearing Problems:**
- **Presbycusis:** Age-related hearing loss
- **Types:** Sensorineural, conductive, mixed
- **Impact:** Social isolation, depression, cognitive decline
- **Management:** Hearing aids, assistive devices, communication strategies

#### Polypharmacy Issues
**Definition:** Use of ≥5 medications simultaneously.

**Risk Factors:**
- **Multiple chronic conditions**
- **Multiple prescribers**
- **Self-medication**
- **Poor communication between providers**

**Problems:**
- **Adverse drug reactions**
- **Drug interactions**
- **Non-adherence**
- **Increased healthcare costs**
- **Functional impairment**

**Prevention Strategies:**
- **Medication reconciliation**
- **Regular medication review**
- **Simplified dosing regimens**
- **Patient education**
- **Use of generic medications**

## Quick Calculation Methods

### Maternal Mortality Ratio (MMR)
**Formula:** MMR = (Number of maternal deaths ÷ Number of live births) × 100,000

**Example:** If there are 50 maternal deaths and 50,000 live births:
MMR = (50 ÷ 50,000) × 100,000 = 100 per 100,000 live births

### Infant Mortality Rate (IMR)
**Formula:** IMR = (Number of infant deaths ÷ Number of live births) × 1,000

**Example:** If there are 100 infant deaths and 25,000 live births:
IMR = (100 ÷ 25,000) × 1,000 = 4 per 1,000 live births

### Under-Five Mortality Rate (U5MR)
**Formula:** U5MR = (Number of deaths under 5 years ÷ Number of live births) × 1,000

**Example:** If there are 120 deaths under 5 years and 25,000 live births:
U5MR = (120 ÷ 25,000) × 1,000 = 4.8 per 1,000 live births

## Clinical Case Scenarios

### Case 1: High-Risk Pregnancy
A 35-year-old primigravida presents at 20 weeks gestation with history of diabetes mellitus and previous abortion.

**Questions:**
1. **Identify maternal risk factors**
2. **What additional investigations are needed?**
3. **Plan for antenatal care**

**Answers:**
1. **Risk factors:** Advanced maternal age, diabetes mellitus, history of recurrent abortion
2. **Additional investigations:** HbA1c, urine culture, retinal examination, fetal anomaly scan
3. **ANC plan:** Frequent visits, strict glycemic control, growth monitoring, fetal well-being assessment

### Case 2: Newborn with Birth Asphyxia
A newborn is born with no spontaneous breathing at birth. Apgar score is 2 at 1 minute.

**Management Steps:**
1. **Immediate drying and stimulation**
2. **Clear airway with bulb syringe**
3. **Position and assess breathing**
4. **Begin bag and mask ventilation**
5. **Continue until spontaneous breathing**
6. **Monitor HR - if <60, start chest compressions**

### Case 3: Elderly Patient with Falls
A 78-year-old woman presents with 3 falls in the past month. She takes 8 medications and has decreased vision.

**Assessment Areas:**
1. **Medication review**
2. **Vision examination**
3. **Gait and balance testing**
4. **Environmental assessment**
5. **Cognitive assessment**

### Case 4: Child with Severe Acute Malnutrition
A 2-year-old child presents with weight 6 kg (expected 12 kg) and bilateral pedal edema.

**Management:**
1. **Admission to hospital (inpatient care)**
2. **Treatment of complications**
3. **Therapeutic feeding with RUTF**
4. **Monitoring weight gain**
5. **Family counseling**
6. **Follow-up after discharge**
